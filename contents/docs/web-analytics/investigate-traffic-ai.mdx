title: Investigate traffic with PostHog AI
---

[PostHog AI](/docs/posthog-ai) analyzes your [web traffic](/docs/web-analytics), investigates anomalies, and builds dashboards using natural language. Ask about traffic sources, bounce rates, user paths, or conversion rates – and PostHog AI queries your web analytics data to find answers.

## How it works

PostHog AI can break down your traffic by any dimension, including:

- **Source and campaign** – organic, paid, referral, direct, and UTM-tagged traffic
- **Geography** – country, region, and city
- **Device** – desktop, mobile, tablet, browser, and OS
- **Pages** – landing pages, exit pages, and navigation paths
- **Time** – hourly, daily, weekly trends and comparisons

## Investigate anomalies

When you notice something unexpected – like a traffic spike or a sudden drop in conversions – PostHog AI can investigate. Ask "why did traffic drop on Tuesday?" and it compares across dimensions to identify what changed.

For example, PostHog AI might find that:

- A specific referral source stopped sending traffic
- Mobile traffic dropped while desktop stayed flat
- A particular landing page's bounce rate spiked

This saves you from manually checking each dimension to isolate the cause.

## Analyze heatmaps

PostHog AI can assess [heatmap](/docs/web-analytics/heatmaps) data for a specific page and recommend concrete changes. Ask "what is my pricing page's heatmap telling me?" and it pulls the interaction data, analyzes it, and returns actionable findings.

Given a page URL, PostHog AI:

- Pulls **click** and **rageclick** hotspots with their coordinates
- Reads the **above/below-the-fold** split and summarizes **scroll depth** (how far 75%, 50%, and 25% of visitors scroll)
- **Cross-references autocapture** clicks to identify the elements under the hotspots – turning coordinates into "clicks on the Pricing link"
- Highlights rage-click clusters, clicks on non-interactive elements, and important CTAs buried below the scroll cliff

You can filter by viewport width to isolate desktop or mobile interactions, and adjust the date range (defaults to the last 7 days, up to 90 days of retained data).

> **Note:** Heatmaps must be enabled in your [project settings](https://app.posthog.com/settings/project-heatmaps#heatmaps) or with the `enable_heatmaps` config in the [JavaScript Web SDK](/docs/libraries/js/config). The heatmap tool also requires [autocapture](/docs/product-analytics/autocapture) to identify clicked elements.

## Tips for better results

- **Name the dimension** – "by source" or "broken down by country" tells PostHog AI which breakdown to apply
- **Ask about specific pages** – "bounce rate on `/pricing`" is more useful than "bounce rates"
- **Compare time periods** – "this week vs last week" or "month over month" gives you trend context
- **Describe the anomaly** – "Why did traffic drop on Tuesday?" prompts PostHog AI to investigate across dimensions
- **Provide the full URL** – heatmap analysis needs an exact page URL including scheme and host (e.g. `https://yoursite.com/pricing`)
- **Specify the device class** – desktop and mobile have different folds, so ask PostHog AI to filter by viewport width for more accurate results
